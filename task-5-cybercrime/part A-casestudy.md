Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers impersonate a trusted organization to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs.
This is usually done through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear legitimate.
A common phishing scenario begins when a student receives an email that looks like it is from their bank or a popular online platform.
The email may claim there is an urgent issue, such as account suspension, and asks the user to click a link to verify their details.
The link redirects to a fake website that closely resembles the original one. When the user enters their login credentials, the attacker captures this information. 
In some cases, the victim may also be asked to enter an OTP, giving the attacker full access to their account.
Students and young adults are frequent targets because they actively use digital platforms and may not always verify the authenticity of such messages.
Once the attacker gains access, they can misuse the account, transfer money, or steal personal data.
The consequences of phishing attacks include financial loss, identity theft, and loss of privacy. 
Victims may also face emotional stress and difficulty in recovering their accounts.
This highlights the importance of being cautious and verifying all digital communications before taking any action.
